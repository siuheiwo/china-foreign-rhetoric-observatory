# China Foreign Rhetoric Observatory — Documentation & Methodology

*A public research observatory tracking how China's official media (People's Daily) signals coercion
toward major powers, 1950–2026. Live, auto-updating daily.*

- **Live site:** https://china-foreign-rhetoric-observator-9xdkvb8yyiscf3maiayzgz.streamlit.app/
  (custom subdomain in progress)
- **Code repository:** https://github.com/siuheiwo/china-foreign-rhetoric-observatory
- **Daily automation:** GitHub Actions → `.github/workflows/daily.yml`

---

## 1. What it is

An interactive dashboard and data pipeline that quantifies, for **12 great powers** (United States, Russia,
Japan, United Kingdom, France, India, Germany, Vietnam, South Korea, Australia, Indonesia, Pakistan), how
strongly *People's Daily* — China's authoritative party newspaper — signals coercion or threat in its
foreign-affairs coverage, over time (1950–present). It is built for researchers and policy analysts. Only
**aggregate** scores (country × time period) are published; **no article text is reproduced** (copyright).

## 2. Data sources

1. **Historical corpus** — ~2.0 million *People's Daily* articles, 1949–2025, each with a Chinese summary
   pre-generated by an LLM (DeepSeek). This underlies all historical scores.
2. **Backfill (Dec 2025 – Jun 2026)** — 17,038 articles scraped directly from the *People's Daily* digital
   edition (`paper.people.com.cn`) to bring the series current; all 194 days, full page coverage.
3. **Daily updates** — a scheduled job scrapes each new day's edition automatically.
4. **External validation benchmark** — the Tsinghua University *China–Great Power Relations Score*
   (Yan Xuetong / 漆海霞), a monthly bilateral-relations index (≈ −9 confrontation … +9 friendly), 1950–2025.

## 3. The indices (what is measured)

Each article is scored on several independent dimensions; articles are then aggregated to country-time means.

| Index | Construct | Method |
|---|---|---|
| **Negativity (tone)** | Overall negativity toward the foreign actor (0 friendly … 1 hostile) | DeepSeek LLM classification of title + summary |
| **Implicit threat (DeepSeek)** | Implied probability that PRC official media signals China will use **force in the coming days** | DeepSeek LLM, multi-shot prompt with canonical threat idioms |
| **Lexicon (19-word)** | Frequency of the validated 19-term implicit-threat lexicon (玩火自焚, 严重后果, …) | jieba tokenization + dictionary count |
| **LSS** | Latent Semantic Scaling on the same construct | jieba tokens; word-embedding scale seeded by the 19 threat terms (+1) vs peace terms (−1) |
| **6 justification frames** | How China *justifies* coercion: legal right · moral/norms · imminent threat · self-defense · limited aims · target-the-few | DeepSeek LLM framing coder (degrees 0–1) |

**Implicit threat ≠ negativity.** Negativity is general tone; implicit threat is *force-signaling
specifically*. They diverge meaningfully (e.g., China's force-signaling tracks relations tightly for the US
but only spiked at the 1969 clash for the USSR).

## 4. Methodology / pipeline (per article)

1. **Scrape** — direct HTTP from `paper.people.com.cn`: enumerate each date's pages
   (`/layout/YYYYMM/DD/node_NN.html`), extract article links, fetch each `content_*.html`, parse
   **date · page · section (版面) · title · author · body text**. (No paid scraping service; the pages are
   plain server-rendered HTML.)
2. **Summarize** — a concise factual Chinese summary (DeepSeek-v4-flash).
3. **Classify** — `foreign` / `china_related` flags, main foreign actor(s), and negativity tone
   (`article_classify_prompt.txt`, verbatim from the original study).
4. **Frame** — the six justification frames (`article_frame_prompt.txt`, verbatim), scored only for
   China-foreign articles (matching the original "涉华涉外" scoping).
5. **Implicit threat** — DeepSeek multi-shot prompt (the 19 threat idioms as recognition cues).
6. **Lexicon + LSS** — jieba tokenization (custom user dictionary keeps idioms like 玩火自焚 as single
   tokens; verified 19/19); lexicon = idiom share of tokens; LSS = mean of fitted term weights.
7. **Aggregate** — to country × {day, week, month}. A period with **0 articles for a country is scored 0**.
   Weekly/monthly means are **article-count-weighted** from the daily series, which exactly reproduces a
   direct per-article mean.
8. **Alarms** — two complementary change-detectors per country (see §6).

## 5. Validation

- **Convergent validity** against the Tsinghua relations score (an independent, established benchmark): the
  implicit-threat indices correlate **negatively** with relations across **all 12 powers** — worse relations
  ⇒ more threat signaling. Yearly examples: DeepSeek US −0.79, France −0.72; LSS mean −0.65; negativity
  US −0.83. Strong support that the measures capture real bilateral dynamics.
- **Discriminant behaviour** — force-signaling is *not* just tone: it tracks relations tightly for the US
  (Taiwan/Korea axis) but only spikes at the 1969 Zhenbao clash for the USSR.
- **Inter-method reliability** — across the four implicit-threat coders, Krippendorff's α ≈ 0.43 (moderate);
  LSS most consistent.
- **Lexicon provenance** — Wong, *Forecasting the Use of Force: A Word Embedding Analysis of China's Rhetoric
  and Military Escalations*, **Political Science Research and Methods**.

## 6. Alarms (how "alarming" is decided)

Two standardized, cross-country-comparable signals, computed per country on the selected index:

- **k-period exceedance (3- and 5-period):** how many standard deviations the recent k-period average sits
  **above that country's own EWMA baseline**. Alert: ≥ 2σ yellow, ≥ 3σ red. (Acute spikes.)
- **CUSUM:** `Cₜ = max(0, Cₜ₋₁ + zₜ − 0.5)`, where `zₜ` is the standardized residual vs a rolling-window
  mean/std. Alert: > 3 yellow, > 5 red. (Sustained drift.)

"More alarming" = higher CUSUM/exceedance, i.e. a country **departing from its own norm** — not simply the
highest raw level (which would always favour chronically-tense relationships).

## 7. Architecture & automation

- **Dashboard:** Streamlit (4 pages) reading small `scores_{daily,weekly,monthly}.csv` + `alarms.csv`.
  Deployed on Streamlit Community Cloud; auto-redeploys on every push.
- **Daily automation:** GitHub Actions (`daily.yml`), cron **08:00 UTC**. Runs `pipeline/daily_update.py`
  (scrape → DeepSeek score → jieba lexicon/LSS → update scores) + `build_alarms.py`, then commits as
  *observatory-bot*. Fully hands-off — no local machine required.
- **Secrets:** `DEEPSEEK_API_KEY` stored as a GitHub repository secret (never in code).

## 9. How to operate / maintain

- **Change app or pipeline code:** edit locally, `git push` (the dashboard redeploys automatically).
  *Workflow files* (`.github/workflows/*`) must be edited via the GitHub **web UI** (the local token lacks
  the `workflow` scope) — use **Add file → Upload files** (uploading preserves YAML formatting; pasting can
  break indentation).
- **Run the pipeline on demand:** Actions tab → *daily-update* → **Run workflow**.
- **Rename the public URL:** share.streamlit.io → app → Settings → Subdomain.
- **If the site shows a stale error:** Streamlit → Manage app → **Reboot**.

## 10. Caveats & limitations

- **Sparsity:** implicit-threat idioms appear in ~2% of articles, so short windows / low-article periods are
  noisy — read levels alongside the article count.
- **Backfill summary step** was **reconstructed** (the original summary prompt was unavailable); the classify,
  framing, and implicit-threat prompts are applied **verbatim**.
- **Tokenization parity:** the daily cloud job uses Python `jieba`; the historical corpus used R `jiebaR`.
  The headline LLM measures are unaffected; lexicon/LSS may differ marginally for new daily data.
- **Event markers** in the trends page are LLM-generated and cross-checked against the Tsinghua score —
  illustrative, **not** a citation-sourced event dataset.
- **Copyright:** source article text is **never** published — only derived aggregate scores.
- **Historical actors** (USSR, East/West Germany) are rendered on present-day borders on the map.
